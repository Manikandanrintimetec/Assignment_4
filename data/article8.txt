On March 18, 2024, the national parliament passed a landmark bill aimed at strengthening data privacy laws. The legislation, approved after months of debate, introduces stricter regulations on how companies collect and use personal data.
Prime Minister Rajiv Mehta described the bill as a “historic step toward protecting citizens’ rights.” The announcement, made on 03/19/2024, was welcomed by privacy advocates and industry leaders.
The new law requires companies to obtain explicit consent from users before collecting their data. It also imposes heavy penalties for violations, including fines of up to $10 million. Experts believe that these measures will encourage organizations to adopt better data protection practices.
Opposition leaders, however, have raised concerns about the potential impact on businesses, particularly small enterprises. They argue that compliance costs may be too high for smaller companies.
Despite these concerns, the government remains confident that the benefits of the law outweigh the challenges. Implementation is scheduled to begin in September 2024.
Public opinion has been largely supportive, with many citizens expressing relief over increased privacy protections. The bill marks a significant shift in the country’s approach to digital governance.
